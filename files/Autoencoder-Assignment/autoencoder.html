Project Overview

This activity introduces students to autoencoders using PyTorch. The goal is to train a neural network that learns to compress and reconstruct handwritten digits from the MNIST dataset.

Students work through a Colab-style notebook where they train an encoder–decoder model, observe reconstruction quality, and experiment with different hyperparameters such as latent dimension, batch size, learning rate, and number of training epochs.

Content

  • MNIST dataset loading and visualization
  • Fully connected autoencoder (encoder + bottleneck + decoder)
  • Training loop using MSE loss
  • Reconstruction comparison (original vs output images)
  • Hyperparameter experiments (latent size, batch size, learning rate, epochs)

What Students Learn

This assignment demonstrates how neural networks can learn compressed representations of data. The encoder reduces the input image into a smaller latent vector, and the decoder reconstructs the original image from this compressed form.

By adjusting model settings, students can observe how compression affects reconstruction quality, highlighting the trade-off between information loss and model capacity.

Suggested Experiments

  • Reduce latent dimension (e.g., 64 → 16 → 8 → 4) and observe reconstruction blur
  • Increase training epochs and compare output quality
  • Adjust learning rate and observe convergence speed
  • Change batch size and observe training stability

Expected Output

Students should be able to visualize side-by-side comparisons of original MNIST digits and their reconstructed versions. The quality of reconstruction depends on the chosen latent space size and training configuration.
